I'm Satowa Kinoshita, a climate strategist and researcher based in San Francisco. 
Over the past decade I've worked at the intersection of climate change, private 
capital, and business strategy — advising startups and investors, managing 
climate-tech investments, and consulting for companies and institutions across 
Asia-Pacific, North America, and Europe.

Most recently I was an Investment Manager at Japan Energy Fund, where I invested 
in climate-tech startups globally working to accelerate decarbonization in Japan 
and beyond. Before that I spent four years at Oliver Wyman as a management 
consultant, advising private equity firms and corporations on strategy and 
financial decisions across financial services and other sectors. I've also worked 
with CrossBoundary on nature-based carbon project finance, and with UNEP-WCMC on 
climate resilience in biodiversity management.

In parallel I've been a Research Fellow at Global Urban Development, supporting 
work on distributed energy resources, regional economic development, and 
sustainable business models.

This fall I'm joining Stanford's Earth Systems Science PhD program, where I'll be 
working with Professor Gabrielle Wong-Parodi. My research interests sit at the 
intersection of climate resilience, human behavior, and decision-making — building 
on the practitioner experience I've accumulated across investment, consulting, and 
advisory work.

I hold a BA and MSci in Natural Sciences (Chemistry) from the University of 
Cambridge and an MBA from the Wharton School. I'm a native speaker of English and 
Japanese, and have written on climate innovation and entrepreneurship for both 
English and Japanese audiences. I also write occasionally on [Substack](https://satowa.substack.com).

Outside of work, I spend time long-distance running, cycling, and skiing; and
making and watching art.
