I'm Satowa Kinoshita, a climate strategist and researcher based in San Francisco.
Over the past decade I've worked at the intersection of climate change, private
capital, and business strategy — advising startups and investors, managing
climate-tech investments, and consulting for companies and institutions across
Asia-Pacific, North America, and Europe.

Most recently I was an Investment Manager at Japan Energy Capital, where I led the
North America presence of a growth-stage venture fund backed by major Japanese
corporates, investing in decarbonization technologies globally. I also advised
Japanese government agencies including NEDO and JETRO on green innovation and
startup acceleration. Before that I spent nearly four years at Oliver Wyman as a
management consultant, advising private equity firms and corporations on strategy
across financial services and other sectors in Asia-Pacific and North America. I've
also worked with CrossBoundary on blended-finance structures for nature-based
carbon projects, and with UNEP-WCMC on climate resilience in biodiversity
management.

In parallel, I've been a Research Fellow at Global Urban Development, supporting
work on distributed energy resources, regional economic development, and
sustainable business models — including as an invited speaker at UC Berkeley's
College of Environmental Design.

This fall, I'm joining Stanford's Earth Systems Science PhD program, working with
Professor Gabrielle Wong-Parodi. My research interests sit at the intersection of
climate resilience, human behavior, and decision-making — particularly how
quantitative modelling and mixed methods can translate complex climate projections
into actionable financial instruments and policy interventions.

I hold a BA and MSci in Natural Sciences (Chemistry) from the University of
Cambridge and an MBA from the Wharton School. I'm a native speaker of English and
Japanese, and have written and spoken on climate innovation and entrepreneurship
for both English and Japanese audiences.

Outside of work I spend time on long-distance running, cycling, and skiing, and
making and watching art.
